The difference

A bot relays commands.
pocket-t is the terminal.

Telegram-bot setups type a command into a black box and hope. You don't see the screen, you can't scroll, you can't approve a tool call mid-run, and it only knows the one shell you wired it to. pocket-t isn't a relay. It's your actual terminal window — every one of them — on your phone.

✕ A Telegram / chat bot
  • — You type a command blind and wait for whatever it decides to echo back.
  • — One wired-up shell. Open a new terminal? The bot has no idea it exists.
  • — No live screen. No scrollback. No cursor. No TUI.
  • — An agent asks to run a tool — it just stalls. You can't approve from your phone.
  • — A wrapper around your shell. Not your shell.

How it works

Three steps. Then never think about it again.

01

Install

One line. A small daemon and a tmux-backed capture layer go on your Mac. Nothing intrusive, nothing in your way.

02

Restart

Restart your Mac once. From that point, every terminal you open is automatically a pocket-t session — with no extra commands, ever.

03

Open your phone

A list of every running terminal. Tap one — see exactly what's happening. Type back. Approve tool calls. Spawn new sessions.

curl -fsSL https://install.pocket-t.ai | sh
pocket-t auth <token>
# restart your Mac — that's the whole setup

Works with

Install

Pick how you run the relay.

The daemon runs on your Mac. The relay is the small public piece your phone reaches. Let us run it, or run it yourself.

curl -fsSL https://install.pocket-t.ai | sh

# 2 — get a token at pocket-t.ai, then

# 3 — restart your Mac, then open

# run the relay on your own box + domain
git clone https://github.com/Josh-Gi3r/pocket-t
cd pocket-t && docker compose up -d

# point the daemon at your relay
POCKET_T_RELAY_URL=wss://your-domain pocket-t auth <token>

FAQ

Straight answers.

How is this different from an SSH app or a Telegram bot?▾
An SSH app gives you one shell you opened by hand. A bot relays single commands into one wired-up process. pocket-t captures every terminal automatically and shows you the real, live, rendered screen of each — with tool-call approvals and two-way input. You're not driving a relay; you're looking at your actual sessions.
Do I have to launch terminals a special way?▾
No. That's the point. After install + one restart, every terminal you open the normal way is already a pocket-t session. No wrapper, no flag, no command to remember.
Is it really free?▾
Yes. The entire product — daemon, relay, apps, encryption — is open source and MIT licensed. You can self-host it forever for nothing. The only thing we'd ever charge for is running the hosted relay so you don't have to.
Is it secure if the relay is public?▾
The relay faces the internet, so it requires authentication — that's the lock on the door. Your phone, your account and your daemon are bound together; a stranger who finds the URL gets nothing. Self-host it and the relay is entirely yours.
What does it work with?▾
Anything that runs in a terminal: Claude Code, Claude CLI, Codex, Aider, Gemini CLI, OpenClaw, Hermes, NanoClaw, Cursor's terminal, Claude Desktop's, plain shells, MCP servers — and whatever ships next, because it captures the terminal, not the tool.
